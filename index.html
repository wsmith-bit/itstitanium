    Quick answer: is it actually titanium?

    For home skillets, the titanium is in the reinforced nonstick, not the base metal. The base is usually aluminum for even heating; the coating includes titanium to improve abrasion resistance. Always check your model’s manual for oven and utensil guidance.

    Care & cleaning essentials

    Heat range

    Use medium for longevity. Hard sears? Grab clad stainless or cast iron instead.

    Utensils

    Silicone or wood protects coatings. Even “metal-safe” benefits from gentler tools.

    Wash cycle

    Let the pan cool, then hand-wash with mild soap and a soft sponge. Dry fully before storage.

    FAQs

    Is titanium cookware the same as titanium-reinforced nonstick?
    No. Pure titanium cookware is common in backpacking gear. Most home “titanium” pans use an aluminum base with a titanium-reinforced nonstick coating for durability.
    Can I sear steaks on titanium-reinforced nonstick?
    Not ideal. Use medium heat for longevity. For hard sears, choose clad stainless or cast iron.
    Dishwasher safe?
    Some are labeled safe, but hand-washing preserves coatings and handle finishes. Check your manual.
    Induction compatibility?
    Only if the base is magnetic. A magnet that sticks firmly is a good sign.
