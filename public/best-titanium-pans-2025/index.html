Care and cleaning quick hits

Heat ramp: Warm the pan on medium for two minutes before cranking heat to prevent thermal shock.

Utensils: Use silicone or wood for daily cooking; reserve stainless tools for metal-tolerant coatings like Scanpan HaptIQ.

Wash: Hand wash with a soft sponge once the pan cools. Skip aerosol sprays that leave polymer film.

Storage: Hang or place a soft liner between pans so rivets from other cookware do not nick the coating.

How to choose a titanium pan in under 15 minutes

  1. Set a budget and weigh the pan. Above 2.5 lb in a 12″ size points to steadier heat without straining your wrist.
  2. Check your stove with a magnet. A quick stick test confirms induction compatibility before you buy.
  3. Inspect the rivets. Smooth hardware speeds wipe-downs and reduces buildup.
  4. Test the release. Fry an egg with a teaspoon of oil—if you need to pry after 15 seconds, the coating is fading.
