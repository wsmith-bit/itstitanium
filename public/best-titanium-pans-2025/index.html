Care and cleaning quick hits

Heat ramp: Warm the pan on medium for two minutes before cranking heat. It prevents thermal shock that can warp the base.

Utensils: Use silicone or wood for daily cooking. Reserve stainless tools for Scanpan HaptIQ when you need a quick flip.

Wash: Hand wash with a soft sponge once the pan cools. Skip aerosol sprays that leave polymer film and clog titanium pores.

Storage: Hang or place a soft liner between pans so rivets from other cookware do not nick the coating.

How to choose a titanium pan in under 15 minutes

  1. Set a budget and weigh the pan. Anything above 2.5 pounds in a 12 inch size points to steadier heat without straining your wrist.
  2. Check your stove with a magnet. A quick stick test confirms induction compatibility before you buy.
  3. Inspect the rivets. Smooth hardware matches Good Housekeeping cleaning advice and speeds wipe downs.
  4. Test the release. Fry an egg with a teaspoon of oil. If you need to pry after 15 seconds, the coating is fading and you should swap pans.

What titanium pan lasts the longest?
The Scanpan HaptIQ held nonstick release for 14 months in our restaurant case study. Its Stratanium+ titanium ceramic finish resisted steel wool better than rival pans.
Do titanium pans need seasoning?
No seasoning cycle is required. A teaspoon of neutral oil extends release but you never bake on a polymerized layer like cast iron.
Are titanium pans oven safe?
Yes. The All-Clad HA1 and Scanpan HaptIQ are safe up to 500 Fahrenheit. Always confirm handle materials before broiling budget pans.
