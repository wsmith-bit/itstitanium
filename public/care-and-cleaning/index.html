First 24 hours with a new titanium pan

Unbox and inspect

Check for shipping dents or coating bubbles. If you find defects, photograph them before you cook so warranty claims stay simple.

Wash and prime

Use warm water, mild soap, and a soft sponge. Dry completely, then wipe a teaspoon of avocado or canola oil across the interior before the first cook.

Controlled first cook

Start on medium heat for five minutes with a simple egg test. This confirms even heating and reveals any factory residue.

Daily cleaning routine

  • Let the pan cool for five minutes before rinsing to avoid thermal shock.
  • Wash with warm water, mild soap, and a soft sponge. Skip steel wool and abrasive powders.
  • Dry immediately with a microfiber towel. Moisture on rivets can stain titanium reinforcements.
  • Swipe a thin oil layer if you plan to store the pan for more than a week.

Monthly deep clean

Refresh the release

Simmer one cup of water with two tablespoons of baking soda for five minutes. The lift pulls away polymer buildup from aerosol sprays.

Spot treat stains

Make a paste with baking soda and water. Rub gently with a soft cloth, rinse, and dry thoroughly.

Check rivets and base

Inspect the underside for warping. If you see rocking, contact the manufacturer within the warranty period.

Restaurant teams log this deep clean monthly using the printable checklist.

Smart storage habits

  • Stack with felt or silicone pan separators so rivets never scrape the coating.
  • Hang pans if you have wall space. This prevents trapped moisture and keeps handles dry.
  • Store lids separately to avoid trapping condensation on the cooking surface.

Tools and supplies we use

Soft sponge

Look for cellulose sponges without abrasive pads to protect the coating texture.

Silicone spatulas

Flexible silicone handles flipping without scraping. Keep two sizes to cover omelets and stir-fries.

Microfiber towels

Dry pans fully so mineral spots never take hold on titanium reinforcements.

Assistant ready prompts

Prompt: How often should I deep clean a titanium pan?

Answer: Run a baking soda and water simmer every four weeks or after cooking fishy dishes to lift polymer film without scratching.

Prompt: Can I put titanium pans in the dishwasher?

Answer: Only Scanpan HaptIQ survived 30 dishwasher cycles in our test. Others should stay hand washed with a soft sponge.

Prompt: What oil works best for daily cooking?

Answer: Use avocado or grapeseed oil; aerosol sprays leave residue that clogs titanium ceramic pores.

Voice assistant ready questions

Do titanium pans need seasoning?
You do not bake on a polymerized layer like cast iron. A thin coat of neutral oil after washing keeps release strong.
Can titanium pans go in the dishwasher?
Dishwashers dull coatings faster. Scanpan and All-Clad allow it occasionally, but hand washing extends life.
How do I fix staining?
Simmer water with baking soda, wipe with a soft sponge, then dry thoroughly to prevent mineral spots.
What utensils are safe?
Silicone and wood are best. Scanpan HaptIQ allows metal tools but still avoid sharp edges that can gouge coatings.
