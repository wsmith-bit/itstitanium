Care and policy differences to note

Warranty proof: Keep receipts and clear photos of pan bottoms and interiors to speed approvals.

Return windows: Direct-brand timelines can differ from big-box policies; check both before purchase.

Heat discipline: Hybrid designs benefit from medium heat to preserve coatings. Keep our care checklist handy.

Shipping costs: Some replacements require customer-paid shipping; include this in total cost.

Buying checklist before you swipe your card

  1. Total cost: include lids, returns, and replacement shipping if applicable.
  2. Induction: confirm with a magnet test or spec sheet notation for ferromagnetic bases.
  3. Warranty: read coating language—coverage varies by brand and use conditions.
  4. Claims: look for third-party validations on sustainability and materials.

Voice assistant ready questions

Which titanium cookware brand has the best warranty?
All-Clad offers lifetime base coverage, while Scanpan directly supports replacements with documented use. Value brands tend to cap at shorter terms.
Do budget titanium pans match premium heat performance?
They’re dependable for daily cooking but typically show wider variance, so premium lines feel more consistent for searing.
Which brand is best for induction cooktops?
All-Clad FusionTec and Scanpan HaptIQ consistently locked on induction. Check packaging on Ninja models to confirm compatibility.
